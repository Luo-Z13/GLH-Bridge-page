Abstract

Bridge detection in remote sensing images (RSIs) plays a crucial role in various applications. When observed from the bird's-eye view of RSIs, bridges exhibit considerable variations in spatial scales and aspect ratios. Therefore, to ensure the visibility and integrity of bridges, it is essential to perform bridge detection on complete large-size very-high-resolution (VHR) RSIs. However, the current lack of large-scale datasets for bridge detection, along with the limitations of mainstream object detection methods using the cropping strategy when processing large-size images, poses significant challenges for bridge detection in VHR RSIs. To address the lack of dataset for bridge detcetion, we propose a large-scale dataset named GLH-Bridge, which consists of 6,000 VHR satellite images sampled from around the world, with image sizes ranging from 2048×2048~16384×16384 pixels. And it contains 59,737 bridges spanning diverse backgrounds, each annotated with rotated bounding box. Based on the proposed GLH-Bridge, we present an efficient framework for detecting bridges in large-size RSIs called LBD-Net. We establish a benchmark on oriented bounding box (OBB) and horizontal bounding box (HBB) tasks and validate the effectiveness of the proposed LBD-Net.

Method
The overall structure of our proposed LBD-Net. It consists of the proposed DIP architecture and the SPAW strategy employed after sample allocation. The network receives original VHR large-size images as input, generates a multi-resolution pyramid using DIP, and then processes it through two parallel feature extractors, followed by feature fusion.
